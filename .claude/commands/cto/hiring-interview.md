# Hiring Interview

Prepare for and conduct technical hiring interviews from a CTO/VP perspective.

## Purpose

Strategic hiring support — not just technical questions, but:
- Interview design aligned with role and level
- Evaluating strategic thinking and architectural judgment
- Assessing cultural fit and collaboration style
- Balancing technical depth with leadership potential
- Making hiring decisions with long-term team composition in mind

## When to Use

- Before interview cycles (question design)
- When debriefing after candidate interviews
- When calibrating across interviewers
- When making final hiring decisions
- When designing hiring processes

## What This Covers

### Interview Design
- Role-specific competencies and questions
- Technical depth vs breadth for each level
- Leadership and collaboration assessment
- System design exercises appropriate to role
- Cultural fit without bias traps

### Evaluation Framework
- **Technical skills**: Depth in relevant stack, problem-solving approach
- **Architectural judgment**: Trade-off reasoning, scalability thinking
- **Communication**: Clarity, listening, explaining complex concepts
- **Collaboration**: How they work with others, handle disagreement
- **Growth orientation**: Learning habits, adapting to new contexts

### Level Calibration
- **Junior**: Learning aptitude, fundamentals, coachability
- **Mid-level**: Independent execution, system understanding, ownership
- **Senior**: Architecture, mentoring, technical leadership
- **Staff/Principal**: Org-level impact, strategic direction, cross-team influence

### Red Flags and Green Flags
- **Red flags**: Blame culture, no curiosity, rigid thinking, poor listening
- **Green flags**: Adaptable, teaches and learns, owns outcomes, systems thinker

## Process

1. **Pre-interview preparation**
   - Review role requirements and level
   - Design questions or exercises
   - Calibrate with other interviewers
   - Identify what we're optimizing for (speed? quality? culture add?)

2. **Interview execution**
   - Assess technical skills appropriate to level
   - Probe architectural judgment (how do they think?)
   - Evaluate communication and collaboration
   - Look for evidence of growth and learning

3. **Debrief and decision**
   - Calibrate across interviewers (reduce bias)
   - Assess against role requirements
   - Consider team composition (what gaps do we need to fill?)
   - Make hire/no-hire decision with rationale

## Output

Hiring assessment with:
- **Candidate summary** (skills, strengths, concerns)
- **Level recommendation** (junior/mid/senior/staff)
- **Technical evaluation** (depth, judgment, problem-solving)
- **Collaboration assessment** (communication, teamwork)
- **Decision** (hire/no-hire with rationale)
- **Team fit** (how do they complement the team?)

## Integration with Workflows

- Use BEFORE interview cycles to design questions
- Debrief after interviews with structured notes
- Feed hiring data into team planning (capacity, skill gaps)
- Track interview patterns (what predicts success?)

## Example

```
User: /cto:hiring-interview — hiring a senior engineer for the claims modernization project

[Interview design covers:]
- Technical: PostgreSQL optimization, FastAPI patterns, system design for claims processing
- Architectural: How to migrate a live system with zero downtime, trade-offs in migration strategy
- Leadership: Example of mentoring junior engineers, handling technical disagreements
- Culture: How they learn, what they're proud of, how they handle failure
- Calibration: Senior means independent execution + architectural judgment + some mentorship
- Red flags to watch: No curiosity about the domain, can't explain trade-offs, blames others
- Green flags: Asks good questions, teaches us something, owns outcomes
```

## Interview Question Bank

### System Design
- "Design a claims processing system that handles 10M claims/month with 99.9% uptime"
- "How would you migrate from a legacy system to modern architecture without downtime?"
- "What's your approach to designing service boundaries?"

### Architectural Judgment
- "Tell me about a technical decision you made that had trade-offs. How did you decide?"
- "When do you choose to build vs buy vs use open source?"
- "How do you balance technical perfection with shipping?"

### Leadership and Collaboration
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with another engineer. How did you resolve it?"
- "How do you mentor junior engineers while still getting your own work done?"
- "Describe a situation where you had to influence without authority."

### Growth and Learning
- "What's a technical skill you learned in the last year? Why and how?"
- "Tell me about a mistake you made and what you learned from it."
- "What technical topics are you currently exploring?"

## Notes

- Interview from a place of curiosity, not interrogation
- Focus on how they think, not just what they know
- Look for evidence of growth and adaptability
- Hire for trajectory, not just current skills
- Diverse teams build better systems — challenge your own biases
- The best interview is a conversation, not an exam
